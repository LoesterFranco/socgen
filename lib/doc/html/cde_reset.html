Theory of Operation

This module is the building block used to build a synchronous reset distribution tree. It provides a means to resync local resets and combine them with a system wide reset. Each reset consists of a pair of signals. One is the active low reset_n that can drive the asynchronous reset port of a flipflip and the other is the active high reset that can drive anything BUT the asynchronous reset port of a flipflop. The local synchronous reset(s) are resynced to ensure that everything is glitchfree. If they are sourced from an asynchronous clock domain then at least two stages of resync logic must be provided to prevent metastability issues.
